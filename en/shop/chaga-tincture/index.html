Raw material

Why is Chaga special?

Chaga (Inonotus obliquus) isn't a mushroom in the classic sense — it grows on living birch trees and can't be cultivated on a substrate. Quality depends directly on the source and method of harvesting the raw material.

The raw material for this product comes from EU/non-EU birch forests. In the GoMushroom process, it then goes through extraction, concentration and final formulation into a tincture.

The point isn't a marketing label of "natural" — it's a deliberately chosen raw material with a clear origin and a controlled processing method.

Quality

GoMushroom Chaga tincture is a dietary supplement made from Inonotus obliquus. It is prepared as a concentrated liquid extract from raw material sourced from birch forests.

It is intended for people looking for a traceable, carefully prepared extract with a clearly defined source of raw material.

The raw material for Chaga comes from EU/non-EU birch forests. Chaga grows on living birch trees and differs from mushrooms that can be classically cultivated on a substrate.

Purified water, Inonotus obliquus mushroom extract, ethanol or vegetable glycerin, sunflower lecithin, vitamin C.

10–20 drops, 2–3 times daily. The tincture can be taken sublingually or diluted in a small amount of water or juice.

Shake the bottle well before use.

The alcohol-based version contains ethanol as part of the final formulation. The alcohol-free version is prepared from the same concentrated extract base, with ethanol and part of the water removed after extraction, then vegetable glycerin added to reach the same final volume.

Because of this, the content of extracted compounds is comparable between both versions.

Concentrated tinctures may naturally show some cloudiness, sediment or slight fraction separation. This is a normal occurrence in products with a higher content of extracted substances.

Do not exceed the recommended daily dose. This dietary supplement is not a substitute for a varied, balanced diet and a healthy lifestyle. Keep out of reach of children.
