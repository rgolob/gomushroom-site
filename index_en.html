Approach — triple extraction & vacuum concentration

We push each species to its maximum — repeatable, traceable and gentle on sensitive compounds.

Step 1

Triple extraction

Ethanol → hot water → pressurized water. Each stage targets a different compound spectrum for a full-profile extract.

Step 2

High solvent volume

Plenty of solvent prevents fast saturation, maintains mass transfer and improves extraction yield.

Step 3

Ultrasound support

Ultrasound micro-cavitation loosens the matrix, accelerates extraction and increases yield.

Step 4

Vacuum concentration

We remove solvents at lower temperatures to better preserve sensitive compounds, plus natural aroma and color.

QC 1

Quality

Internal QC for raw materials and finished products: dry matter, density, extractable fraction and batch traceability.

QC 2

Compliance

Work follows HACCP principles and a GMP approach; ethanol use is managed with appropriate traceability and records.

About Rok Golob — GoMushroom

Rok Golob — GoMushroom

My name is Rok Golob and I’m behind the GoMushroom project. By education I’m “almost a chemical engineer” — my studies in chemical engineering ended just shy of the finish line. But the mindset that chemistry and process technology brings is with me every day: logic of steps, understanding solvents, mass flows, and above all, respect for details.

I’ve been working in an analytical laboratory for nearly 20 years — and I still do analytical work today in my regular job. Analytics teaches you quickly: a result is only good if it’s repeatable, if the method makes sense, if you understand sampling and the matrix, and if your records are clean. There’s no room for approximations. That discipline is exactly why GoMushroom didn’t start as a quick trend, but as a process-driven project built on practice and systems.

GoMushroom grew out of curiosity and a desire to make extracts the way I personally want them: no inflated promises, clear process logic, and a focus on stability. Small batches allow tight control — from raw material to the final bottle — and help keep each batch comparable to the previous one.

What pulls me in most is the “engineering side”: designing a process that is efficient, gentle to sensitive compounds, and repeatable. When it all comes together — raw material, process, control points, measurements and documentation — the result is not luck-dependent, but system-dependent. That’s the essence of GoMushroom.
